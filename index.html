Choosing Your Wines

A neutral side-by-side of every wine on the list, scored for how well it fits your two Gold-menu dishes and how much a big, mixed room of guests will enjoy it. Pick one white and one red — the shortlists at the end make it easy.

White pairs with →

Sablefish & Prawns

Sablefish (black cod) is a rich, buttery, silky fish; sweet prawns alongside. The miso marinade adds a savoury, lightly sweet, umami glaze.

Wants: a white with some body and texture to match the richness, plus fresh acidity to keep it lively. Aromatic and lightly-oaked styles love miso.

Red pairs with →

Charbroiled Rack of Lamb

Cooked medium, char off the grill, finished with a savoury demi-glace. A classic, hearty red-meat plate.

Wants: a medium-to-full red with enough structure to stand up to the meat, but smooth enough tannin that casual drinkers still enjoy it.

How to read the tables.  Sweetness and Body run left (dry / light) to right (sweeter / fuller). Tannin (reds) is that drying, grippy feel — higher can taste harsh to newcomers. Crowd-appeal is how broadly a room of mixed guests tends to like it: Wide almost everyone, Mixed loved by many but divides some, Niche for keen drinkers. Fit is the pairing match with your dish. Price is per bottle (pre-tax/service), straight from the list.

★ Easy white pick for a wedding

CedarCreek Pinot Gris ($59)  ·  or Ken Forrester Chenin Blanc ($59)

Both have enough body to sit beside the rich sablefish, keep a fresh streak so they don't feel heavy, and are the kind of easy-drinking whites almost nobody dislikes. Great value in the middle of the list.

Want a touch more special? Mission Hill Reserve Chardonnay ($66) is the safe crowd-Chardonnay, or the Joie Noble Blend ($67) is a delicious aromatic wildcard that's spectacular with miso (just slightly off-dry, so taste it first). Splurge: William Fevre Petite Chablis ($90).
Steer clear if you want zero risk: the big California Chardonnays (La Crema, Rodney Strong) — lovely to some, "too buttery" to others.

★ Easy red pick for a wedding

Catena Malbec ($69)  ·  or E. Guigal Côtes du Rhône ($62)

Both are medium-full, smooth, and savoury — genuinely made for grilled lamb — yet soft enough that guests who "don't usually drink red" will happily finish a glass. The Guigal is also one of the best values on the whole list.

Prefer the softest, safest option? Mission Hill Reserve Merlot ($74) or Rodney Strong Merlot ($60) — plush and almost impossible to dislike. More elegant/savoury: Villa Antinori ($81) or Cedar Creek Meritage ($69). Bolder, if your crowd likes big reds: Mission Hill Reserve Syrah ($86).
Save the Barolo, Brunello and big Cabernets for a dinner with fewer, keener wine drinkers — their firm tannins can taste harsh to casual guests.
